Design Challenge

An important step in this process was defining what the Design Challenge was. I used the questions from the IDEO Human Centered Design Toolkit to help me define my design challenge.

What is the problem you're trying to solve?

Stopping animal cruelty in upstate South Carolina.

Frame it as a design question.

How can the Animal Rescue Fund of South Carolina's website encourage people to help stop animal cruelty?

Now, state the ultimate impact you're trying to have.

The goal is to get more people to visit ARF's website, as well as encourage, and hopefully increase, donations and volunteers.

What are some possible solutions to your problem?

Cleaning up the website, making the navigation bar more concise and clear, having a footer with the location and contact information, hierarchy, a simpler design, donation button in the nav bar, get rid of links that aren't pertinent to the cause, easier to navigate, appeal to a wide demographic.

Finally, write down some of the context and constraints that you're facing.

It's a local animal rescue group. Relatively unknown organization compared to others. It needs to be easy for a wide demographic to navigate, fit to all screens. Also, some people only donate to big, well-known non-profits like ASPCA.

Does your original question need a tweak? Try it again.

How can the redesign of the AFR website make navigation easier and encourage more donations, volunteers, and adoptions.
